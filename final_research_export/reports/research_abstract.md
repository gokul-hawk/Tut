# Abstract

**Title:** Multisignal Graph Knowledge Tracing (MGKT): A Neuro-Symbolic Approach to Pedagogical Mastery Detection

Traditional Knowledge Tracing (KT) models, such as Bayesian Knowledge Tracing (BKT) and Deep Knowledge Tracing (DKT), predominantly rely on binary correctness logs ($0/1$) to infer student mastery. This "signal blindness" treats a lucky guess on a multiple-choice question as equivalent to a rigorously implemented code solution, often leading to inflated mastery estimates (false positives) and a failure to recognize deeper conceptual understanding. Furthermore, deep learning-based approaches (DKT, GKT) suffer from the "Cold Start" problem, requiring extensive interaction histories before making accurate predictions.

To address these limitations, we propose **Multisignal Graph Knowledge Tracing (MGKT)**, a neuro-symbolic architecture that integrates explicit pedagogical priors with a dynamic graph attention mechanism. Unlike standard models, MGKT differentiates between interaction types—weighting **Debugging (Analysis)** and **Coding (Synthesis)** significantly higher than **Quiz (Recall)** responses. We introduce a **Graph Attention (GAT)** propagation mechanism that leverages the domain's prerequisite structure to "warm-start" readiness scores for dependent concepts, allowing the model to infer mastery in related topics without direct observation.

We evaluated MGKT against BKT, DKT, and standard GKT using a comprehensive simulation of 80 distinct student trajectories across four pedagogical scenarios ("Lucky Guesser", "Instant Expert", "Deep Struggle", "Inconsistent Student"). Our results demonstrate that MGKT achieves a **36% reduction in false positive mastery attribution** for "Lucky Guessers" and an **88% improvement in convergence speed** for "Instant Experts" compared to baseline models. Most notably, in "Deep Struggle" scenarios where students oscillate before a breakthrough, MGKT’s ability to detect debugging success led to a **117% increase in sensitivity** to true mastery (the "Eureka Moment"). These findings suggest that incorporating multisignal evidence and graph-based priors offers a more robust, interpretable, and data-efficient alternative to black-box neural networks for intelligent tutoring systems.

**Keywords:** Knowledge Tracing, Neuro-Symbolic AI, Graph Neural Networks, Intelligent Tutoring Systems, Bayesian Inference, Pedagogical Modeling.
